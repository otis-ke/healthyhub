Bengali cuisine is unique in the ingredients it has. The dishes retain much of the fibre and a lot of proteins are eaten in every meal cooked with healing spices.

One of the most celebrated cuisines in the world is Bengali cuisine. Everything about Bengali cuisine is unique from the rest of the cuisines across the globe.

Those lip-smacking delicacies complete with mustard seeds and paste, special 5 spice mix panch phoran, river fish curries and milk-based desserts are what make the Bengali spread drool worthy.

Parboiled Rice

Bengalis prefer to eat parboiled rice to ensure they get a good dose of B vitamins. In parboiling, unmilled rice is boiled or steam-heated for a short time, retaining more B vitamins than plain white rice.

Fish

Fish is a high protein food that is low in calories and fat. The omega-3 fatty acids found in fish can reduce the risk of heart disease and ensure proper brain and eye development. Try Tangra Macher Jhol and Ilish Macher Jhol!

Lentils

Lentils (Dal) are a must in Bengali Cuisine. They are low in calories, rich in protein, and provide more folic acid than any other food. Soluble fiber in lentils decreases blood glucose and cholesterol.

Bitter Gourd (Korola)

Bitter melons are rich in iron, effective in treating diabetes, and beneficial for people with autoimmune diseases. Bitter gourd is used in many Bengali dishes like stews and bhorta.

Turmeric is used in nearly all Bengali dishes and is excellent for the skin. It corrects metabolism, helps digest protein, and has strong antiseptic properties.

Poppy Seeds

Poppy seeds are a powerhouse of calcium and natural coolants that help relieve stress. Try Aloo Potol Posto.

Garlic

Garlic is an effective antibiotic, antiviral, and antifungal agent, and may reduce LDL cholesterol. It's used in nearly all Bengali dishes.

Onion

Onions are rich in vitamin C and fiber, increase circulation, lower blood pressure, and prevent blood clotting. Necessary for cooking almost all types of curries, dals, meat, and fish in Bengali cuisine.

Ginger

Ginger is used heavily in preserves, chutneys, vegetable, and meat dishes. It treats colds, coughs, indigestion, and more.

Green Papaya

Green papaya is a strong digestive aid and promotes healthy skin. It's an excellent source of vitamins and antioxidants that prevent cancer.
